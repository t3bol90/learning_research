# Learning Research

## Motivation of this repository

1. Research training aimed at lab postgraduates and undergraduates. The hope is to write down research experience rather than passing it on by word of mouth, so that newcomers to the lab can learn how to do research.
2. By making this experience open-source, it may also help students outside the lab.
3. Open-sourcing the material lets others review it and offer suggestions, which then improves the advice over time.

**Postscript:**
1. The contents may not fully capture what I want to say, and may not be complete. If you want to learn research deeply, the recommendation is to use these notes as a reference and learn through practice and conversation.
2. The document is based on [my own](https://pengsida.net/) research experience in [our lab](https://xzhou.me/), and may not apply to other situations.
3. The document will keep being updated (updates may happen in Notion, so they will not always show up in the commit history). Last updated: 8 May 2026.

## How to work towards becoming a top PhD student

<!-- To put together this summary of research experience, the first question to answer is: what abilities does a top PhD student have? -->

The core ability: in my view, a top PhD student knows how to set a long-term research goal. The goal should have important scientific value and practical value (finding genuinely valuable scientific problems within real applications). From that goal, they break the research roadmap into smaller pieces. The few papers produced during the PhD all sit around solving this goal, and together they show the student's progress along the roadmap clearly. The paper demos should be as cool as possible (example: [a model PhD student, Sebastian Starke](https://pengsida.notion.site/Sebastian-Starke-1713fe292ff1808eb33be93ea2d79ad9?pvs=4)).

In day-to-day research work, a PhD student needs five abilities: finding important research problems, proposing solutions, running experiments, writing papers, and giving presentations. Below are some specific suggestions.
