# Whether to do research and how to choose a direction

### How to choose a research direction

Pick a direction you are good at and that gives you strong positive feedback. You will start to like it soon enough.

Do not get too tangled up over what your interests are. Most people find it hard to know what they truly enjoy. You might think you like a particular direction at first, but if positive feedback never comes, the interest will fade.

### Whether to do research

1. The first tier, the people most suited to research: those who get enjoyment out of the work itself. Driven by interest, and good at doing it.
2. The second tier, also suited to research: people who are good at it and for whom research helps meet some personal goals.
3. The third tier, still suited to research: people who are good at it but do not yet have a clear goal.
4. People not suited to research: those with weak fundamentals (a gap somewhere, such as poor expression, weak understanding, or weak programming) and who are not good at the work. Over time, they get no positive feedback.
