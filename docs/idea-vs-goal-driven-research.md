# Idea-driven vs goal-driven research

> Document index (GitHub repo): [https://github.com/pengsida/learning_research](https://github.com/pengsida/learning_research)

What is idea-driven research: make X work better.
> Pick a direction, read a paper, come up with an idea that does better than that paper.

What is goal-driven research: work for a goal.
> Set a clear goal you want to reach (new AI capabilities), then plan a roadmap of important tasks that gets you to that goal, then knock the tasks off one by one and update the roadmap as you go.

<details>
<summary>Pros and cons of idea-driven research</summary>

Pros: easy to start. This is probably how most newcomers do research.

Cons:
1. You are following someone else's work, so unless your improvement is large, the paper has limited impact.
2. It is easy to clash with others on the same idea, since other people may also be improving the same paper.
3. There are already solutions in place, so the room for innovation in the method is small. You need to be very smart and have deep insight to propose a breakthrough new technique for the task. (Quite hard.)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Pros and cons of goal-driven research</summary>

Pros:
1. Easier to produce breakthrough innovations, because you are leading rather than following up.
2. Your research has stronger motivation and a bigger picture, which helps attract others.
3. The research is more continuous and forms a system.
4. You can organise a team to tackle the tasks on the roadmap separately.

Cons:
1. Planning a good roadmap is hard.
2. It is easy to end up with task-specific techniques. You need to push for techniques that are as general as possible, so they can be used in other areas as well.

</details>
