<summary>How to train these abilities through a research project, <strong>how to do a research project</strong></summary>

| Things a research project includes | How to carry these out (what these tasks involve) | Abilities these tasks train |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Think of an important task | 1. [Read large numbers of papers to build a vision for an area.](./literature-tree.md)<br>2. [Think about the big picture of this research direction.](./getting-advanced/coming-up-with-ideas.md)<br>3. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Reading:** read papers in the relevant research direction, and the ability to build a literature tree<br>**Innovation:** the ability to come up with ideas, and the ability to do goal-driven research<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
| 2. Think of the technical challenges or failure cases that need to be solved | 1. [Read large numbers of papers to find out what problems papers in this area are trying to solve.](./literature-tree.md)<br>2. [Think about which problems are important in this research direction.](./getting-advanced/coming-up-with-ideas.md)<br>3. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Reading:** read papers in the relevant research direction, and the ability to build a literature tree<br>**Innovation:** the ability to come up with ideas, and the ability to do goal-driven research<br>**Practice:** find out, through experiments, which problems a technique can solve and which it cannot<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
| 3. Think of solutions to the problems | 1. [Read large numbers of papers to find out what techniques papers in this area have proposed for the matching problems.](./literature-tree.md)<br>2. Read papers in a targeted way, and see whether any papers (especially papers from other research directions) are solving a high-level technical challenge similar to ours. (**You can ask your advisor or fellow students whether such papers exist.**)<br>3. Design solutions targeted at the problems or failure cases you have raised. | **Reading:** read papers in the relevant research direction, and build a challenge-insight tree<br>**Innovation:** the ability to design new pipelines |
| 4. Sort out the logic of the solution clearly | 1. Sort out the solution you have proposed.<br>2. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Innovation:** the ability to design new pipelines<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
| 5. Judge whether the solution is correct and novel | 1. Sort out the solution you have proposed.<br>2. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Innovation:** the ability to validate new pipelines<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
| 6. Design simple experiments to quickly verify that the solution is correct | 1. Design experiments that quickly verify your solution.<br>2. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Practice:** coding ability, experimental ability, the ability to find out why an experiment isn't working<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
| 7. Improve the solution | 1. [Analyse experimental phenomena and find out why an experiment isn't working.](./getting-advanced/why-experiment-isnt-working.md)<br>2. Read related papers in a targeted way and see what tricks they propose to make a technique work. (**You can ask your advisor or fellow students whether such papers exist.**)<br>3. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Practice:** coding ability, experimental ability, the ability to find out why an experiment isn't working<br>**Reading:** read related papers, track and accumulate techniques<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
| 8. Brainstorm more candidate solutions | 1. [Analyse experimental phenomena and find out why an experiment isn't working.](./getting-advanced/why-experiment-isnt-working.md)<br>2. [Read related papers and see whether they have other solutions for similar problems.](./literature-tree.md) (**You can ask your advisor or fellow students whether such papers exist.**)<br>3. Design new solutions.<br>4. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Practice:** coding ability, experimental ability, the ability to find out why an experiment isn't working<br>**Reading:** read related papers, build a challenge-insight tree<br>**Innovation:** propose new techniques<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
| 9. Run experiments to make the solution work | 1. [Find out why an experiment isn't working, and so improve the idea.](./getting-advanced/why-experiment-isnt-working.md)<br>2. Read related papers and see what tricks they propose to make a technique work. (**You can ask your advisor or fellow students whether such papers exist.**) | **Practice:** coding ability, experimental ability, the ability to find out why an experiment isn't working<br>**Reading:** read related papers, build a challenge-insight tree |
| 10. Think about what to do next | 1. Plan what experiments to run next. | **Practice:** coding ability, experimental ability, the ability to find out why an experiment isn't working |
| 11. Sort out clearly what to do next | 1. Plan what experiments to run next.<br>2. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Practice:** coding ability, experimental ability, the ability to find out why an experiment isn't working<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
| 12. Set the important deadlines for your paper based on the paper's submission deadline (comparison experiments, ablation study, introduction writing, method writing, abstract writing, experiment writing, related work writing) | 1. Plan what to do for the paper.<br>2. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Presentation:** writing ability, presentation ability, the ability to make a paper look polished, the ability to make demos<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
| 13. Write the paper | 1. [Write the paper, and at the same time think about how to make appealing experiments and demos.](./paper-writing/template.md) | **Presentation:** writing ability, presentation ability, the ability to make a paper look polished, the ability to make demos |
| 14. Review the paper (list missing experiments and existing writing problems) | 1. [Review your own paper, think about which other experiments are still needed, and find existing writing problems.](./paper-writing/reviewing-papers.md)<br>2. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Presentation:** writing ability, presentation ability, the ability to make a paper look polished, the ability to make demos<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
| 15. Revise the paper | 1. [Review your own paper, think about which other experiments are still needed, and find existing writing problems.](./paper-writing/reviewing-papers.md)<br>2. [Talk to your advisor and fellow students.](./getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md) (Notion) | **Presentation:** writing ability, presentation ability, the ability to make a paper look polished, the ability to make demos<br>**Communication:** the ability to talk and discuss things with advisors and fellow students |
