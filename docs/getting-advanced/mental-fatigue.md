### Original article title: Why do I get tired so easily?

<!-- zh: 特别容易疲劳，是因为大脑后台(可以类比为手机、电脑后台)持续进行着无意识地担忧，白白消耗能量。 -->
You get tired so easily because the background processes in your brain (think of it like the background apps on your phone or computer) keep worrying without you noticing, burning energy for nothing.

<!-- zh: 换句话说，吃大米饭转化的能量没干正事，大部分都用来担心害怕了。 -->
Put another way, the energy you got from eating rice never goes to anything useful. Most of it is spent on worry and fear.

<!-- zh: 你有没有这样的体验？ -->
Have you had this experience?

<!-- zh: 工作时，如果你特别投入专注，并且很顺利，自己虽然干了很多活，但也并不觉得累。（甚至可能很享受） -->
When you are deeply focused at work and things are going smoothly, you might get a lot done and still not feel tired. You might even enjoy it.

<!-- zh: 可如果工作不顺利，自己非常焦虑，虽然没干什么，但晚上下班回家还是觉得累。 -->
But when work goes badly and you feel anxious, you barely do anything, yet you still feel exhausted by the time you get home in the evening.

<!-- zh: 这是什么呢？因为大脑后台，又叫DMN在作祟！ -->
What is going on? It is the [brain's background system](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E5%A4%A7%E8%84%91%E5%90%8E%E5%8F%B0&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2015298724%7D), also called the DMN, doing its work behind the scenes.

<!-- zh: DMN，又叫默认网络系统，可以类比为电脑（或手机）后台（运行着各种软件或app）。 -->
The DMN, also known as the [default mode network](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E9%BB%98%E8%AE%A4%E7%BD%91%E7%BB%9C%E7%B3%BB%E7%BB%9F&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2015298724%7D), is similar to the background of a computer or phone, where various programs and apps keep running. [1]

<!-- zh: 它由腹侧内侧前额皮质、背内侧前额皮质、后扣带回皮质和临近的楔前叶及外侧顶叶皮质组成。它在社会行为、情绪控制、动机驱动有关的感觉-内脏运动联系中发挥着潜在作用，对一个人的性格与行为有着重要影响。 -->
It is made up of the ventromedial prefrontal cortex, the dorsomedial prefrontal cortex, the [posterior cingulate cortex](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E5%90%8E%E6%89%A3%E5%B8%A6%E5%9B%9E%E7%9A%AE%E8%B4%A8&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2015298724%7D), and the nearby precuneus and lateral parietal cortex. It plays a role in the sensory-visceral motor links tied to social behaviour, [emotion regulation](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E6%83%85%E7%BB%AA%E6%8E%A7%E5%88%B6&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2015298724%7D), and motivation, and it shapes a person's personality and behaviour in important ways.

![](https://picx.zhimg.com/80/v2-99e3ecb646719b454be6b92e5e66cfda_1440w.webp?source=2c26e567)

<!-- zh: 致使我们疲劳的是 DMN 自发认知的特性，即当我们不想思考时，头脑中还会出现各种杂念。 -->
What tires us out is the spontaneous cognition that the DMN produces. Even when we do not want to think, all sorts of stray thoughts still pop up.

<!-- zh: 例如上班时，你很想专注工作，但大脑除了会思考工作内容外，还会时不时冒出 "中午吃啥？"、"刷会儿微博"等等的想法。 -->
For example, while you are at work and trying to focus, your brain will also throw out random ideas like "what should I have for lunch?" or "let me scroll Weibo for a bit."

<!-- zh: 思考工作任务，是大脑的主动思考。而"中午吃什么"、"刷会儿微博"则是DMN的自发认知（念头）。 -->
Thinking about your work task is active thought. "What should I eat for lunch" and "let me scroll Weibo" are spontaneous thoughts produced by the DMN.

<!-- zh: 令人震惊的是，由 DMN 主导的自发认知消耗大脑能量的 60% 左右，而大脑执行具体任务的能量仅消耗 5 %。 -->
Here is the striking part. Spontaneous cognition driven by the DMN consumes about 60% of the brain's energy, while carrying out specific tasks only takes about 5%.

<!-- zh: 这就像打王者荣耀时，觉得手机卡，等打开后台，发现居然开着十几个app。最想玩的游戏（使用的软件），只占据了手机内存很小的一部分，其它内存都被无用app占据了。所以游戏卡，手机也很累。 -->
It is like playing Honour of Kings and finding the phone laggy. You open the background and see more than a dozen apps running. The game you actually want to play takes up only a small slice of memory; the rest is taken up by useless apps. So the game lags and the phone is worn out too.

<!-- zh: 同时研究人员发现，DMN 的能源消耗大部分来自于对过去和未来的担忧。（头脑后台大部分想法，都是与过去和未来的担心相关） -->
Researchers have also found that most of the DMN's energy use comes from worrying about the past and the future. Most of what runs in the background of your mind has to do with worry about the past or the future.

<!-- zh: 相比于 "中午吃什么"这类容易觉察的想法，DMN（大脑后台）不停产生的想法可能是 "我的工作太繁重了，怎么办？"、"男朋友会不会不爱我了"、"(小时候)妈妈骂了我，我觉得自己好笨啊"....... -->
Compared with easy-to-notice thoughts like "what should I eat for lunch," the DMN keeps churning out things like "my workload is too heavy, what should I do?", "what if my boyfriend doesn't love me anymore?", or "mum scolded me when I was little and I felt so stupid"...

<!-- zh: （担忧的想法，平时不易觉察，但可能一直在大脑后台运转） -->
Worried thoughts are usually hard to notice, but they may be running in the background of the brain the whole time.

<!-- zh: 平时我们应该都有这样的体验，当自己特别焦虑的时候，即使什么不做也很累，这里的能量消耗就是担忧的想法造成的。 -->
We have all had this feeling. When you are very anxious, you feel tired even when you are doing nothing. That drain in energy is what the worried thoughts cost you.

<!-- zh: 而大脑不像手机，我们不可能像关掉手机后台一样，关掉 DMN 的自发认知。所以减缓疲劳的最佳方式，就是减少担忧。具体可以怎么做呢？ -->
The brain is not like a phone. You cannot shut off the DMN's spontaneous cognition the way you close background apps. So the best way to ease the fatigue is to worry less. How can you actually do that?

### 1. Come back to the present

<!-- zh: 你可以做冥想。Judson 等研究人员，将参与者分为两组，一组进行冥想（将注意力集中到呼吸，不试图改变呼吸的方式，注意力游走就重新温柔地带回呼吸），对照组大脑正常运转。 -->
You can [meditate](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E5%86%A5%E6%83%B3&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2015298724%7D). Judson and colleagues split participants into two groups. One group meditated, putting their attention on the breath without trying to change it, and gently bringing the attention back to the breath whenever it wandered. The control group let their brain run as usual. [2]

<!-- zh: 冥想后，冥想组 DMN 的两个重要节点（ PCC 和mPFC ）活动少于对照组，冥想组参与者也报告不受控制的思考减少，能量消耗也自然减少了。 -->
After meditating, two key nodes of the DMN in the meditation group, the PCC and the mPFC, were less active than in the control group. People in the meditation group also reported fewer uncontrolled thoughts, and naturally used less energy.

<!-- zh: 也可以跑步。村上春树的写作效率很高，同时每天都会跑步，在《我在谈跑步时我谈些什么》他提到： -->
You can also go running. Haruki Murakami writes a lot and also runs every day. In [What I Talk About When I Talk About Running](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E6%88%91%E5%9C%A8%E8%B0%88%E8%B7%91%E6%AD%A5%E6%97%B6%E6%88%91%E8%B0%88%E4%BA%9B%E4%BB%80%E4%B9%88&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2015298724%7D) he writes: [3]

<!-- zh: > 我跑步，只是跑着。原则上是在空白中跑步，也许是为了获得空白而跑步。即便在这样的空白当中，也有片时片刻的思绪潜入。这是理所当然的，人的心灵中不可能存在真正的空白。 -->
> I run; I simply run. As a rule I run in a void, or perhaps I run in order to get to that void. Even within that void, thoughts slip in for brief moments. That is only natural. There can be no true void in the human mind.

<!-- zh: 村上春树跑步的体验和冥想相似，都是从日常的担忧中跳脱出来，回归当下，获得真正的休息。 -->
[Murakami](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E6%9D%91%E4%B8%8A%E6%98%A5%E6%A0%91&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2015298724%7D)'s experience while running is similar to meditation. Both pull you out of daily worries, bring you back to the present, and give you real rest.

### 2. Write down your worries and reappraise them

<!-- zh: 担忧并不总是客观的，例如季度末你工作压力很大，觉得很累。不妨先将自己的担忧写下来。写下的担忧可能是： -->
Worries are not always objective. For example, at the end of the quarter you are under a lot of work pressure and feel tired. It helps to write the worries down first. What you write might look like:

<!-- zh: "工作完成不了"、"我可能被辞退" …… -->
"I won't finish the work," "I might get fired"...

<!-- zh: 接着对担忧的想法进行重新评估。 -->
Then reappraise those worries using [cognitive reappraisal](https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E8%AE%A4%E7%9F%A5%E9%87%8D%E8%AF%84&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2015298724%7D).

<!-- zh: "工作完成不了" 这个想法是客观的么，还只是自己的担忧。对工作任务进行重新规划，或许就可以完成。 -->
Is the thought "I won't finish the work" actually true, or is it only worry? If you replan the tasks, you may be able to get them done.

<!-- zh: "被辞退" 的可能性有多大呢，或许只有 5%、10%，基本不可能发生。目前只要尽可能努力完成，实在完成不了也可以找人帮忙。 -->
How likely is "getting fired"? Maybe 5% or 10%, basically not going to happen. For now, just put in your best effort, and if you really cannot finish, you can ask someone for help.

<!-- zh: 一旦我们意识到头脑中不可控的担忧，并重新思考和评估后，担忧的程度就可能下降，进而降低能量消耗。 -->
Once you notice the uncontrolled worries in your head and rethink and reassess them, the level of worry can drop, and so does the energy you spend on it.
