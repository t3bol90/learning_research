# Defining natural science: maths vs science

<!-- zh: 转载自：[https://hr.edu.cn/xueshu/202209/t20220908_2244759.shtml](https://hr.edu.cn/xueshu/202209/t20220908_2244759.shtml) -->

<!-- zh: 科学起源于数学，数学早于科学产生（注：这里的科学特指自然科学）。5000多年前，四大文明古国和古希腊都产生了数学，公元前300年左右，古希腊数学蓬勃发展，产生了真正成体系的欧几里得几何学。当数学蓬勃发展的时候，产生了科学的萌芽，而科学则是产生于14世纪中叶至17世纪初在欧洲发生的思想文化运动后，在伽利略等人的努力下制定了科学研究的规范，才产生真正意义上的科学。用我们今天定义的数学和科学来区分年代的话，科学只有400多年的历史，而数学却有2000多年的历史。 -->
Science grew out of maths, and maths came first (note: "science" here means natural science specifically). More than 5,000 years ago, the four ancient civilisations and ancient Greece all produced mathematics. Around 300 BCE, Greek maths flowered into the first truly systematic body of knowledge: Euclidean geometry. While maths was flourishing, the early shoots of science appeared. But science in any modern sense only emerged after the European intellectual and cultural movements of the mid-14th to early 17th centuries, when Galileo and others laid down the rules of scientific research. By the definitions we use today, science is only about 400 years old, while maths has more than 2,000 years of history behind it.

<!-- zh: 研究对象不同。数学是用符号语言研究数量和空间关系，研究对象可以是实的，也可以是虚的，具有很强的抽象性。科学则是研究自然界物质的现象和事物的发生、发展和变化规律，既包括物体个体、物体系统、物体细分，也包括宏观现象、微观现象等，科学研究的对象必须是实的，具有很强的实证性。 -->
They study different things. Maths uses a symbolic language to study quantity and spatial relations, and what it studies can be real or imagined, which makes it highly abstract. Science studies physical phenomena in nature and the rules by which things arise, develop, and change. It covers individual objects, systems of objects, and their subdivisions, as well as both large-scale and small-scale phenomena. What science studies has to be real, which makes it strongly empirical.

<!-- zh: 研究方法不同。数学比较注重逻辑推理，2000多年前的数学家们就确定了"大胆猜测，严密论证"的演绎论证方法。所有数学定理全部要经过演绎论证，否则不可以进入严密认证自洽的数学体系。科学则侧重于实验，是基于假设，推理的摸索与探索，自然科学的所有学科都是注重实验、观察的科学，从可重复的实验中得出的结论。 -->
They use different methods. Maths leans on logical reasoning. More than 2,000 years ago, mathematicians settled on the deductive method of "bold conjecture, rigorous proof." Every mathematical theorem has to be proved deductively, otherwise it cannot enter the tight, self-consistent body of mathematics. Science leans on experiment. It feels its way forward through hypothesis and inference. Every branch of natural science is built on experiment and observation, and conclusions are drawn from repeatable experiments.

<!-- zh: 结论的可靠性不同。数学定理一般非常可靠，等同于真理，不易被后来者推翻。科学上的结论往往只是一个时代的真理，随着数学理论的更加完善，实验和检测手段的不断提高，其它学科提供了更加充分的证据，使得有些前人认为是真理的东西被证明不是自洽的，科学结论过了它所处的时代可能就不再可靠了。 -->
Their conclusions hold up differently. Mathematical theorems are usually very reliable. They count as truths, and later generations rarely overturn them. Scientific conclusions are often only the truth of an age. As mathematical theory becomes more refined, as experimental and measurement methods improve, and as other fields supply better evidence, some things earlier generations took for truth are shown to be inconsistent. A scientific conclusion that holds in its own time may not hold in the next.

<!-- zh: 数学是科学的先锋。马克思曾明确指出"一种科学只有在成功运用数学时，才算达到了真正完善的地步"。数学为科学研究提供了工具，数学推理为科学探索提供了指引和借鉴。只有数学发展到更高水平，科学才能上升迈上新的理论体系。如在物理学领域，当数学发展水平处于欧几里德几何学时期，科学研究只能建立在静止力学的基础上，对应的科学体系是托勒玫的"地心说"；牛顿发明了微积分，科学研究才可能在动态力学的基础上进行，逐步建立了哥白尼—牛顿的科学体系；当数学发展到非欧几里德几何学阶段，爱因斯坦以发展演变的动态宇宙观，用黎曼几何推演，才发现了广义相对论，建立了当今的爱因斯坦—霍金的科学体系。 -->
Maths is the vanguard of science. Marx put it plainly: "A science only reaches a state of true completeness when it successfully applies mathematics." Maths gives science its tools, and mathematical reasoning gives scientific inquiry its guidance and its reference points. Only once maths has reached a higher level can science climb to a new theoretical system. Take physics. When the level of mathematics was Euclidean geometry, scientific research could only stand on static mechanics, and the matching scientific system was Ptolemy's geocentric model. When Newton invented calculus, scientific research could move onto a foundation of dynamic mechanics, and the Copernicus-Newton system was built up step by step. When maths reached the stage of non-Euclidean geometry, Einstein, working from a dynamic and evolving view of the universe, used Riemannian geometry to derive general relativity and built today's Einstein-Hawking system.

<!-- zh: 科学的好奇和探索推动数学不断向前发展，在科学发展过程中，也给数学提出一些新的课题。量子力学是在20世纪初由一大批科学家共同创立的，创立初期所用的数学是线性代数，彻底改变了科学家对物质组成成分的观点。量子力学研究了差不多一百年，特别是对量子纠缠的研究，有些现象既无法用代数来描写，也无法用分析来推演，由于数学发展水平的限制，至今无重大突破，并没有改变大众对物质组成成分的认知。量子力学没有取得突破，是因为没有现成可用的数学方法，急需要发明新的数学理论和方法。 -->
The curiosity and inquiry of science also push maths forward, and along the way science hands maths new problems to solve. Quantum mechanics was created in the early 20th century by a large group of scientists working together. The maths they used at the start was linear algebra, and it completely changed how scientists thought about what matter is made of. Quantum mechanics has now been studied for nearly a hundred years. In the work on quantum entanglement in particular, some phenomena can be described neither by algebra nor by analysis. Because mathematics has not yet caught up, there has been no major breakthrough, and the public's view of what matter is made of has not really changed. Quantum mechanics has not broken through because there is no ready-made mathematical method for it. New mathematical theories and methods are urgently needed.

<!-- zh: 数学与科学有着天然的联系。现代科技的发展得益于数学的发展，可以说几乎所有科技领域都用到数学，数学用地越好，科技水平和技术含量就越高。数学认知能力的发展是人类探究和解决问题的前提，人类解决问题，从宏观到微观，从宇宙到地球，所有的探索都离不开数学。 -->
Maths and science are linked by their nature. Modern science and technology owe their progress to the progress of mathematics. You could say almost every field of science and technology uses maths, and the better the maths is used, the higher the technical level. Building up our mathematical understanding is what lets human beings investigate and solve problems in the first place. From the large scale to the small, from the cosmos to the Earth, every line of inquiry runs through maths.
