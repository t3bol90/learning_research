# Exploratory experiments should follow minimum viability

<!-- zh: ### 最小可行性是什么 -->
### What minimum viability means

<!-- zh: 科研中的最小可行性是指用最少的资源、时间和精力，构建一个刚好能验证核心科学假设或技术原理的简化版实验、模型或原型。 -->
In research, minimum viability means using the smallest amount of resources, time, and effort to build a stripped-down experiment, model, or prototype that is just enough to test the core scientific hypothesis or the technical principle behind it.

---

<!-- zh: ### 最小可行性的好处 -->
### Why minimum viability helps

<!-- zh: **核心重要性：降低风险，提高效率** -->
**Core point: lower the risk, raise the efficiency.**

<!-- zh: 这是最小可行性（MV）最根本的价值。科研本质上是探索未知，充满了不确定性和失败的风险。 -->
This is the most basic value of minimum viability (MV). Research is, at its heart, an exploration of the unknown, and it is full of uncertainty and the risk of failure.

<!-- zh: - 快速验证假设，避免方向性错误：你可能有一个非常巧妙的想法，但其中可能隐藏着致命的逻辑缺陷或未被考虑的实际约束。投入大量时间精力建造一个复杂的实验系统后才发现根本行不通，是巨大的浪费。MV方法让你能快速做一个简单实验，先回答"这个想法原则上是否可能？"这个问题。如果简单实验都失败了，你可以果断放弃或转向，节省了大量宝贵资源。 -->
- Test your hypothesis quickly so you do not commit to the wrong direction. You may have a very clever idea, but it can hide a fatal logical flaw or some real-world constraint you never thought about. Pouring a lot of time and effort into a complex experimental setup, only to find out the whole thing does not work, is a huge waste. With an MV approach you run a simple experiment first and answer the question, "Is this idea possible in principle?" If even the simple experiment fails, you can cut your losses or pivot, and save a lot of valuable resources.

<!-- zh: - 高效利用有限资源：科研资源（经费、设备、学生时间、计算资源）永远是稀缺的。MV迫使你聚焦于最核心的问题，避免在项目早期就陷入优化细节、构建完美系统的陷阱。把好钢用在刀刃上。 -->
- Use limited resources well. Research resources, whether money, equipment, student hours, or compute, are always scarce. MV forces you to focus on the most central question and keeps you out of the trap of optimising details or building a perfect system early in the project. Spend the good steel on the cutting edge of the blade.

<!-- zh: - 快速获得反馈，迭代推进：MV产出的是一个"虽然简陋但能工作"的初版。你可以用它来收集初步数据，向导师、同行展示，申请预研经费，或者发现设计中的问题。基于这些早期反馈，你可以进行有针对性的迭代和改进，使研究路线始终沿着最高效的方向演进。这是一个"构建-测量-学习"（Build-Measure-Learn）的循环。 -->
- Get feedback fast and iterate. What MV produces is a first version that is rough but works. You can use it to collect initial data, show it to your advisor and peers, apply for pre-study funding, or find problems in the design. Based on this early feedback you can iterate and improve in a targeted way, keeping the research path on the most efficient line. It is a Build-Measure-Learn loop.

<!-- zh: ### 做探索性实验具体怎么执行最小可行性的规则 -->
### How to apply the minimum viability rule when running exploratory experiments

<!-- zh: **核心思想：以最小成本验证想法的可行性。** -->
**The core idea: test the feasibility of an idea at the smallest cost.**

<!-- zh: **第一步：定义"可行性"的标准（明确目标）** -->
**Step 1: define what "feasibility" means (set the goal).**

<!-- zh: 在开始任何实验之前，你必须清晰地回答：这个最小实验要验证什么？成功的标准是什么？ -->
Before you start any experiment, you have to answer clearly: what is this minimum experiment meant to verify, and what counts as success?

<!-- zh: 这个"可行性"标准必须是二元的、明确的、可测量的（是/否，达到/未达到）。它就是你这次探索的"罗盘"。 -->
This "feasibility" standard has to be binary, explicit, and measurable (yes or no, met or not met). It is the compass for this round of exploration.

<!-- zh: **第二步：设计"最小"的实验（极致简化）** -->
**Step 2: design the "smallest" experiment (simplify to the extreme).**

<!-- zh: 这一步的任务是剥离所有非必要因素，构建一个能回答第一步中核心问题的最简单系统。 -->
The job here is to strip out everything that is not necessary and build the simplest system that can answer the central question from Step 1.

<!-- zh: 具体执行方法： "如果我现在只有一天时间，我能做什么最简单的实验来检验我的想法？" -->
A practical way to do it: ask yourself, "If I only had one day, what is the simplest experiment I could run to check my idea?"

<!-- zh: **第三步：执行与学习（快速行动，聚焦反馈）** -->
**Step 3: run it and learn (act fast, focus on feedback).**

<!-- zh: 1. 快速执行： 目标是尽快完成实验，拿到原始数据。不要追求完美无瑕的实验记录（但要保证可重复），接受一定的"粗糙度"。 -->
1. Run it fast. The goal is to finish the experiment as soon as possible and get the raw data. Do not chase a flawless lab notebook (but keep it reproducible) and accept a degree of roughness.

<!-- zh: 2. 分析结果，对照标准： 将得到的数据与第一步设定的"可行性标准"进行严格对比。 -->
2. Analyse the results against the standard. Compare the data you got strictly against the feasibility standard you set in Step 1.

<!-- zh: 3. 做出明确的决策： 结果只导向三个清晰的行动方向： -->
3. Make a clear decision. The result only points to three clear directions for action:

<!-- zh: 	- 成功（假设得到支持）： 证明核心想法是可行的。下一步是：设计一个更严谨、更大规模的实验来巩固发现。你可以开始增加复杂度，优化细节。 -->
	- Success (the hypothesis is supported). The core idea is feasible. The next step is to design a more rigorous, larger-scale experiment to lock in the finding. You can start adding complexity and tuning details.

<!-- zh: 	- 失败（假设被推翻）： 核心想法不成立。这是一个巨大的成功！ 因为你用最小的成本避免了一个大坑。下一步是：深刻分析失败原因，是想法根本错误，还是实现方式有问题？基于此，果断 pivot（转向） 到一个新的想法，或者终止这个方向。 -->
	- Failure (the hypothesis is overturned). The core idea does not hold. This is a huge win, because you avoided a big pit at minimal cost. The next step is to dig into why it failed: was the idea fundamentally wrong, or was the implementation off? Based on that, pivot decisively to a new idea, or end this direction.

<!-- zh: 	- 不确定/数据模糊： 结果既不支持也不推翻假设。这通常意味着实验设计得不够好，"最小可行性"的"可行性"标准没设定清楚。下一步是：重新审视第一步，优化实验设计，让它能给出更明确的答案。可能需要一个更"精炼"的最小实验。 -->
	- Unclear or fuzzy data. The result neither supports nor refutes the hypothesis. Usually this means the experiment was not designed well and the "feasibility" criterion in your minimum-viability setup was not clear enough. The next step is to go back to Step 1, refine the design, and get it to give a more definite answer. You may need a more polished minimum experiment.
