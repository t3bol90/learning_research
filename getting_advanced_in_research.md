# Building your research ability

Writing a paper mainly calls for three abilities: coming up with ideas (finding important research problems and proposing solutions), writing (presentation), and running experiments.

1. [How to build the ability to come up with ideas](https://pengsida.notion.site/idea-da6ce171c13846b7a7ffaa7473ffa6ea).
2. Build writing and presentation skills through [reading papers](https://pengsida.notion.site/d192db870bc64436ae4a4a590b36772a), [weekly meeting slides](https://pengsida.notion.site/meet-ppt-d697ef578d784c869d4f8314f0d617da), [paper writing](https://pengsida.notion.site/c1a22465a0fa4b15a12985223916048e) (including [practising paper writing](https://pengsida.notion.site/c13c7e52aab64c1a8e3576b97fcb9851)), and presenting your paper (slides, videos, demos).
3. Build experimental skills through writing code to run experiments, [working out why an experiment isn't working](https://pengsida.notion.site/1aee6e718de6472f834d13da8f4ff097), and [keeping experiment notes](https://pengsida.notion.site/caf34717f4c046c69ee7e14ea953c46f).

I have laid out the abilities a PhD student should have, and how a research project can help build each one, in a table in [this document](https://pengsida.notion.site/research-project-b43507ef26d044bd888ac29f4736e116) (**recommended reading**). The same document also covers how to run a research project.
