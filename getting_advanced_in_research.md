# Building your research ability

Writing a paper mainly calls for three abilities: coming up with ideas (finding important research problems and proposing solutions), writing (presentation), and running experiments.

1. [How to build the ability to come up with ideas](./docs/getting-advanced/coming-up-with-ideas.md).
2. Build writing and presentation skills through [reading papers](./docs/getting-advanced/reading-papers.md), [weekly meeting slides](./docs/getting-advanced/weekly-meeting-slides.md), [paper writing](./docs/paper-writing/template.md) (including [practising paper writing](./docs/paper-writing/practising.md)), and presenting your paper (slides, videos, demos).
3. Build experimental skills through writing code to run experiments, [working out why an experiment isn't working](./docs/getting-advanced/why-experiment-isnt-working.md), and [keeping experiment notes](./docs/getting-advanced/keeping-experiment-notes.md).

I have laid out the abilities a PhD student should have, and how a research project can help build each one, in a table in [this document](./docs/research-project.md) (**recommended reading**). The same document also covers how to run a research project.
